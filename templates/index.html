Why Gender Matters in Climate Action?

Gender inequality affects different communities experience and respond to climate change. Women and marginalized genders often bear a disproportionate burden in the face of environmental challenges.

Women make up a large percentage of the agricultural workforce, yet they are often excluded from decision-making roles. Ensuring gender-sensitive policies in climate action can create more resilient communities and foster sustainable solutions.
